## 📋 **PRESENTATION TIPS**

### For Person 1 (Introduction):

- **Energy level:** HIGH – you're selling the vision
- **Body language:** Expansive gestures, eye contact with audience
- **Voice:** Clear, passionate, varied pace
- **Goal:** Make them CARE before they see features

### For Person 2 & 3 (Demo):

- **Energy level:** FOCUSED – you're proving it works
- **Body language:** Point to screen, trace UI elements
- **Voice:** Confident, technical but accessible
- **Goal:** Show EVERY feature works, not just mockups
- **Tip:** Practice transitions between tabs so they're smooth

### For Person 4 (Closing):

- **Energy level:** INSPIRING – you're painting the future
- **Body language:** Confident stance, open posture
- **Voice:** Slower, more dramatic than Person 1
- **Goal:** Make them REMEMBER your project after they leave

### Overall Timing:

- **Introduction:** 2-3 minutes
- **Demo Part 1:** 4-5 minutes (Tabs 1-3)
- **Demo Part 2:** 4-5 minutes (Tabs 4-7)
- **Closing:** 2-3 minutes
- **TOTAL:** 12-16 minutes
- **Q&A:** 5-10 minutes

### Pro Tips:

1. **NO APOLOGIES** – Don't say "this is just a prototype" or "we didn't have time to..."
2. **PRACTICE TRANSITIONS** – Person-to-person handoffs should be smooth
3. **BACKUP PLAN** – If demo breaks, have screenshots ready
4. **WATER BREAKS** – Especially for demo people (dry mouth from talking)
5. **REHEARSE** – Run through this at least 3 times before presenting
